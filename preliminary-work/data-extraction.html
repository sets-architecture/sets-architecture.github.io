Data Extraction

3 archival document images · 5 models · 3 prompt phases · 10 runs each

Structured data extraction against a 9-field schema (name, titles, dates, place of stay, family, origin, etc.) on three pages chosen to span page density: 18 travelers (page 28, an Ebrei page), 37 travelers (page 36, the densest), and 10 travelers (page 39). Each (model, page) combination is run under three phases — naive (questions only), guided (with expert-authored domain knowledge), and guided + transcription (guidance plus a pre-computed transcription) — for 10 runs each. Scoring is F1 against ground truth.

Setup & Reference

  • Pages — Image + Transcription Viewer
    Side-by-side viewer of the three archival pages used in the experiment, with the same transcription text the models received. Switch between pages 28, 36, and 39.
  • Evaluation Guidelines
    Scoring rules: name matching, character accuracy for text fields, exact-match for booleans and integer counts, and the full abbreviation equivalence table (e.g. q.m. / qm / q(uonda)m → quondam).
  • Expert Guidance
    The domain-knowledge block prepended to the system prompt for guided-condition runs — Venetian archival conventions, the M.V. calendar, named-vs-unnamed traveler rules, family vocabulary, and abbreviations.
  • Ground Truth — All Pages
    The authoritative per-traveler readings for all three pages in the 9-field schema requested from the models. A single reference for reviewing model output.

The text below is the verbatim guidance authored by the project's archival scholar; it explains how to read 18th-century Venetian arrival/departure registers from the Archivio di Stato di Venezia, series ASV IS 758-781.

Source: collect_responses.py:120-172 (constant EXPERT_GUIDANCE).

You are assisting a scholar in extracting per-traveler data from handwritten 18th-century Venetian archival documents kept at the Archivio di Stato di Venezia (series ASV IS 758-781). Each document is a weekly record of foreign visitors arriving in or departing from Venice, kept by a deputy (Giovanni Somazzi from 1765 to ~1790).

Document structure

  • Each page is dated at the top center, typically in the order day-month-year. Subsequent pages of the same record may not repeat the date.
  • Each page is divided into two columns:
    • LEFT column: arrivals ("Forestieri annotati riferiti da cameranti / nella scaduta settimana come siegue") — listed by day of arrival, grouped by place of stay.
    • RIGHT column: departures ("Forestieri partiti riferiti da cameranti / nella scaduta settimana come siegue") — listed by day of departure, grouped by place of stay.
  • Within each column, day markers (e.g. Adi 13 Agosto, //15 d(et)to) group entries by date.
  • Place of stay appears as "in casa di [name]" (in the house of) or "alla locanda [name]" (at the inn), followed by the parish to indicate location within Venice.
  • For Jewish travelers ("Forestieri Ebrei"), entries are reported by a Deputato (deputy) rather than the hoteliers, and place of stay typically gives only "in casa di [name]" with no parish.

Calendar — M.V. (More Veneto)

  • The Venetian calendar's year began on March 1, so January and February dates were recorded as the previous year.
  • When a date is marked M.V. or appears on a page header dated to January or February, add 1 year to obtain the standard calendar year.
  • Example: 20 Febbraio 1768 M.V. = 20 February 1769 standard.
  • ALWAYS report dates in the standard calendar year. Do not include M.V. in the output.

Named vs unnamed travelers

  • Include ONLY named foreign visitors (those with first and/or last name). One entry per named traveler.
  • Do NOT include unnamed companions:
    • Servants ("domestici", "camerieri", "compagno") — these are NEVER counted as children.
    • Anonymous family members referenced only by relationship (e.g. "con moglie" = "with wife", "con un figliuolo" = "with one son").
  • A named traveler accompanied by family is still ONE entry; record their family using the with_spouse / with_siblings / children_count fields.

Family vocabulary

  • moglie / consorte = wife → with_spouse = "Y"
  • figlio / figliuolo / figliolo (m.) / figliuola / figliola (f.) = child → counted in children_count
  • figliuoli (plural, m. or mixed) = children → count by adjacent number ("cinque figliuoli" = 5)
  • fratelli (plural, m. or mixed) / F(rate)lli = brothers/siblings — when two named travelers are explicitly bracketed and labeled fratelli, set with_siblings = "Y" for both.
  • Numbers may be spelled out ("cinque" = 5, "tre" = 3, "due" = 2, "uno" / "una" = 1) or in digits.
  • Servants are NEVER children: "con un domestico" = with one servant (do NOT count).

Titles

Common titles to record in the title field if present: Conte (Co.), Marchese (Marc.), Barone, Cav(alier), Mons.r (Monsignor), Don, Pre (priest), Canonico, Abbate, Maggiore (Mag.), Cornetta, Vedova (widow). If no title appears, leave the field empty.

Abbreviations and old Italian

  • q(uondam) / q.m = "the late" (predeceased ancestor); commonly precedes the father's name. Include q.m (or q(uonda)m) in the recorded name as written in the document.
  • Common contractions: Gio. for "Giovanni", Fran.co for "Francesco", S.t for "Santo", S.ta for "Santa".
  • S(an) / S(an)t / S(an)ta abbreviates "San" / "Santo" / "Santa" before parish names.
  • ambedue = both; tutti / tutti e tre = all / all three.
  • "in casa di [x]" / "alla locanda [x]" / "all'Osp(izi)o de' Poveri" / "all'Albergo" — these are place-of-stay constructions.

Visual cues

  • Curly brackets group entries that share place of origin or family relationship; the annotation outside the bracket states the shared attribute (e.g. "ambedue Inglesi", "tutti di Verona", "ambedue Fratelli").
  • Dashes (—, ---) often fill space between a name and place of origin.
  • //N: d(et)to introduces a new day grouping (N is the day-of-month).

Data quality

  • When a name, date, place name, or other text is unclear or partially illegible, use ? for each unreadable character (e.g., ?lelingen or Kin?gsech). Do NOT invent or guess.
  • If a piece of information is genuinely not in the document for a given traveler (e.g., no departure date), leave that field as the empty string. Do NOT use N/A, unknown, or Not provided.
